The Challenge: Declining Strip Mall Performance

Cherry Creek Retail Plaza, a 45,000 square foot strip center in Denver's affluent Cherry Creek area, was struggling with declining foot traffic, high tenant turnover, and sub-optimal tenant mix despite its prime location.

Critical Issues:

  • 73% occupancy rate with 8 vacant units out of 28 total
  • 35% tenant turnover annually due to poor sales performance
  • Foot traffic down 42% over 3 years despite neighborhood growth
  • No data-driven insights into customer behavior patterns
  • Tenant complaints about poor cross-shopping and customer flow

Management was signing any tenant willing to pay rent, regardless of fit with the center's customer base. This reactive approach created a disjointed mix of businesses that failed to create synergies or attract sustained foot traffic.

The PropertyPilot Solution: Smart Traffic Analytics

PropertyPilot deployed an AI-powered foot traffic analysis system that transformed how Cherry Creek Plaza understood and optimized its customer experience.

Advanced Traffic Monitoring

Computer vision cameras and sensors throughout the center tracked:

  • Visitor Patterns: Entry/exit points, dwell times, and movement flows
  • Demographics: Age, gender, and group composition analysis
  • Shopping Behavior: Store visits, cross-shopping patterns, and conversion tracking
  • Peak Times: Hourly, daily, and seasonal traffic variations
  • External Factors: Weather, events, and local business impact on traffic

AI-Powered Tenant Optimization

Machine learning algorithms analyzed traffic data to optimize tenant mix and positioning:

  • Tenant Performance Scoring: Correlation between foot traffic and sales per tenant
  • Cross-Shopping Analysis: Which tenant combinations drive the most traffic
  • Location Optimization: Ideal placement for different business types
  • Market Gap Identification: Missing tenant categories that would attract more visitors

Implementation Strategy

The transformation was executed through a data-driven, phased approach over 8 months.

Phase 1: Infrastructure & Baseline (Months 1-2)

  • Installation of 32 computer vision cameras and 15 sensor nodes
  • 3 months of baseline data collection and pattern analysis
  • Integration with existing security and POS systems

Phase 2: Analysis & Strategy (Months 3-4)

  • Comprehensive traffic flow analysis and customer journey mapping
  • Identification of underperforming spaces and optimal tenant categories
  • Development of tenant mix optimization strategy

Phase 3: Tenant Mix Optimization (Months 5-8)

  • Strategic tenant replacement and repositioning
  • Addition of anchor tenants in high-traffic locations
  • Layout modifications to improve customer flow

Key Strategic Insights:

  • Anchor Placement: Food & beverage tenants drove 67% more cross-shopping
  • Traffic Magnets: Service businesses (salon, spa) generated longer dwell times
  • Complementary Mix: Fashion + beauty + wellness created powerful synergies
  • Peak Optimization: Extended hours for high-traffic tenants increased overall visits
