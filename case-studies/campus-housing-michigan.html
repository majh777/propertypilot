The Challenge: High-Volume Maintenance Nightmare

Wolverine Commons, a 2,400-bed student housing complex at the University of Michigan, faced overwhelming maintenance demands from heavy usage patterns and aging infrastructure.

Critical Issues:

  • $3.4M annual maintenance costs with 34% year-over-year increases
  • Average 48-hour response time for non-emergency repairs
  • 865 monthly work orders during peak academic periods
  • Student satisfaction scores dropping due to facility issues
  • Maintenance staff burnout from constant reactive firefighting

With 18 residence halls housing undergraduate and graduate students, Wolverine Commons struggled to maintain reliable operations while controlling escalating costs in Michigan's harsh climate conditions.
