Implementation and Migration

Migration Timeline

  • Week 1: Data export and cleanup from current system
  • Week 2: New system setup and configuration
  • Week 3: Data import and verification
  • Week 4: Team training and parallel operations
  • Week 5: Full cutover and go-live

Data Migration Considerations

  • Clean up duplicate and outdated records before migration
  • Verify all tenant contact information and lease details
  • Ensure financial data accuracy and reconciliation
  • Test integrations with banks and payment processors
  • Create backup procedures for critical data

Common Implementation Mistakes

1. Insufficient Planning

Rushing implementation without proper planning leads to data issues and user adoption problems. Take time to map workflows and clean data.

2. Inadequate Training

Invest in comprehensive team training to ensure successful adoption. Most software failures are due to poor user training, not software limitations.

3. Over-Customization

Avoid extensive customization that complicates upgrades and maintenance. Use standard features when possible.

4. Ignoring Integration Needs

Consider how the software will integrate with your existing tools, accounting systems, and business processes.

Making the Final Decision

Trial Period Strategy

  • Test with a subset of your portfolio
  • Involve actual users in the evaluation process
  • Test all critical workflows and integrations
  • Evaluate customer support responsiveness
  • Compare total cost of ownership, not just subscription fees

Questions for Vendors

  • What is your data migration process and timeline?
  • How do you handle customer support and training?
  • What integrations are available for our existing tools?
  • How often do you release updates and new features?
  • What is your uptime guarantee and backup procedures?
